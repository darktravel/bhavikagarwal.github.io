About
Hello! I am a thesis-track M.S. student in Computer Science at Johns Hopkins University, advised by Prof. Casey Overby Taylor, and a Machine Learning Research Scientist at MasterControl. I work on dependable small language model systems, including multi-agent orchestration, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), knowledge-graph–augmented retrieval, and parse-safe structured generation (e.g., ThinkJSON) for regulated domains such as genomics, pharmacogenomics, and compliance.

Previously, I completed a degree in Computer Science at IIIT Delhi (Dean’s List), worked as a Senior AI/ML Engineer at BlueStacks / now.gg on large-scale traffic forecasting, and as an AI/ML Scientist Intern at SAP Labs on multi-agent embedding-space API orchestration for SAP Joule. I have also built Azure-based RAG systems for genomics and pharmacogenomics in collaboration with Microsoft Research Azure Support and Johns Hopkins, and was a research mentee in the Google DeepMind CS Research Mentorship Program.

My primary research focus is on small, tool-using language models with strong guarantees: models that can reliably call tools, ground answers in knowledge graphs and document stores, and obey strict output schemas (JSON/programs) in safety-critical workflows. I am particularly interested in evaluation and training schemes that tie model outputs to verifiable evidence and constraints, so systems can be trusted in regulated environments like healthcare and compliance.

Outside work, I enjoy playing table tennis, watching movies and anime, and reading. I also like mentoring students interested in NLP/LLMs and discussing M.S./Ph.D. application strategies—feel free to reach out via email.
